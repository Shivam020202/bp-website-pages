Digital marketing strategies for your auto manufacturing companies

Though there are many digital marketing techniques available, these four work especially well with the auto manufacturing industry.

Search engine optimization (SEO)

Search engine optimization, or SEO, is the practice of making your website easily readable to Google, and beneficial for your readers. Making your website easy for Google to understand makes it easier for customers to find you online.

When a user searches for something on Google, it returns results based on what their algorithm has determined to be the most relevant and beneficial pages for the information they want.

With SEO, you can improve your rankings in results for keywords and phrases related to your business, so that more searchers will see and visit your website.

The higher your pages rank in search results, the better. In fact, 33% of users will click the first result, and less than 10% of users will even advance to the second page of results. This explains why SEO is a crucial part of your marketing strategy.

However, achieving the rankings you want involves several steps, and an ongoing SEO strategy.

As you work through these steps and create your strategy, you should strive to create the best site experience possible for your target audience.

For example, if you are a Jeep manufacturer, and after some keyword research you find that a high volume of users are asking what kind of Jeep is best for off-roading, you should create a page dedicated to answering that question.

Or if you find that there is a high search volume for the keyword ‚Äúbike rack for [your car brand],‚Äù you could create a page about where to find those bike racks, and why they can be an asset to your vehicle.

Pay-per-click advertising (PPC)

PPC is a kind of ad platform, and is also known as pay-per-click advertising.

You can place PPC ads in search engines like Google and Bing.

The ads work on a bidding system ‚Äì so you choose the keywords you want to target with your ads, and then choose how much you are willing to pay every time the ad is clicked. If your bid is among the highest for a given keyword, your ad will show up when a user searches it.

PPC is extremely budget-friendly, too, because you only pay when the ad is clicked ‚Äì not for the ad space alone.

Social media is primarily used for improving brand awareness, and you can share industry news and share your own website‚Äôs content, in order to spark conversation with your followers.

Your website is often the first impression you make on potential customers, so if it‚Äôs unorganized, difficult to navigate, or poorly designed, your target audience could develop a negative opinion of your brand.

This means that your website should be an accurate representation of your company. For example, if you‚Äôre dedicated to creating trucks that make it through the toughest conditions, your website could utilize darker, more industrial colors, and use graphics that give a rugged feel.
